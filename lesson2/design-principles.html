Design Principles Examples

Visual Hierarchy

This principle is all about organizing the elements of a design in a way that guides the user's eye to the most important information first.

Google

Google homepage uses visual hierarchy to guide the user's eye to the most important information first. The search bar is the largest and most prominent element on the page, followed by the Google logo and the two main buttons, "Search" and "I'm Feeling Lucky." The rest of the content on the page is arranged in a way that is less visually prominent, but still easy to read and scan.

White Space and Clean Design

Emphasizes the importance of using empty space to create a sense of balance and clarity in a design.

Apple

The Apple homepage is a great example of how to use white space and clean design to create a sense of balance and clarity. The page is predominantly white, with only a few key elements highlighted in black. This makes it easy for the user to focus on the most important information, such as the product images and the call-to-action buttons.

PARC: Alignment

Elements that are aligned with each other are perceived as being related.

Airbnb

Airbnb homepage uses alignment to create a sense of order and unity. The main elements of the page, such as the search bar, the location filter, and the list of listings, are all aligned with each other. This makes it easy for the user to find and navigate the information they are looking for.
